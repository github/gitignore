Cosmic Riddle

"I'm a force that sparks change without a fight,
speeding reactions in the dead of night.
In space, I could launch a ship or end a war –
what am I, from this vocab lore?"

Answer: Catalyst ✨

Arbitrary

Definition: (Adj.) unreasonable; based on one's wishes or whims without regard for reason or fairness

Synonyms: capricious, high-handed, autocratic

Antonyms: reasoned, rational, objective, equitable

Examples:
  • A judge may be criticized for rulings that appear to be arbitrary and without legal precedent.
  • I don't know why I chose that one; it was a completely arbitrary decision.

Catalyst

Definition: (N.) a substance that causes or hastens a chemical reaction; any agent that causes change

Synonyms: stimulus, spur, instigator

Antonyms: N/A

Examples:
  • Enzymes are catalysts that aid in the digestion of food.
  • The bombing attack was the catalyst for war.

Facilitate

Definition: (V.) to make easier, to assist

Synonyms: ease, smooth the way, simplify

Antonyms: hamper, hinder, obstruct, impede

Examples:
  • The Federal Reserve Board may lower interest rates in order to facilitate economic growth.
  • Her rise to power was facilitated by her influential friends.

Incorrigible

Definition: (Adj.) not able to be corrected; beyond control

Synonyms: unruly, intractable, incurable, inveterate

Antonyms: tractable, docile, curable, reparable

Examples:
  • Criminals deemed incorrigible can expect to receive maximum sentences for their offenses against society.
  • He is always the class clown, and his teachers say he is incorrigible.

Militant

Definition: (Adj.) given to fighting; active and aggressive in support of a cause; (n.) an activist

Synonyms: (adj.) truculent

Antonyms: (adj.) unassertive, peaceable, passive

Examples:
  • In the struggle for civil rights, Martin Luther King, Jr., advocated peaceful rather than militant protest.
  • Elizabeth Cady Stanton was a militant in the fight for woman suffrage.

Paramount

Definition: (Adj.) chief in importance, above all others

Synonyms: supreme, primary, dominant

Antonyms: secondary, subordinate, ancillary

Examples:
  • The safety of the passengers is paramount to the airline industry.
  • The patient's well-being was the doctor's paramount concern during the surgery.

Rebut

Definition: (V.) to offer arguments or evidence that contradict an assertion; to refute

Synonyms: disprove, confute, shoot holes in

Antonyms: confirm, corroborate, substantiate

Examples:
  • He was able to rebut the accusations made against him with evidence of his innocence.
  • Attempts to rebut the myths could be easily batted away as a case of no smoke without fire.

Reprimand

Definition: (V.) to scold; find fault with; (n.) a rebuke

Synonyms: (v.) reproach; (n.) reproof

Antonyms: (v.) pat on the back

Examples:
  • You'll get a five thousand pound fine and a severe reprimand for having police lights on your car in the UK.
  • When I misbehaved in school, my teacher sent me home with a written reprimand for my parents to sign.

Servitude

Definition: (N.) slavery, forced labor

Synonyms: bondage, thralldom

Antonyms: liberty

Examples:
  • My daughter has to work in servitude until she can earn enough money to replace the big-screened television she broke.
  • In the past, attempts to commit murder were punishable by penal servitude for life.

Slapdash

Definition: (Adj.) careless and hasty

Synonyms: cursory, perfunctory, slipshod

Antonyms: painstaking, thorough, in-depth

Examples:
  • We ran out of time and had to use a slapdash solution that failed to fix the problem.
  • Her slapdash approach to studying resulted in a poor grade on the exam.
